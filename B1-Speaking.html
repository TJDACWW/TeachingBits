Welcome & Grade A Goal

Use these short activities in the waiting room to warm up your English and get into “exam mode” before your B1 Speaking test.

Your goal: Grade A in B1 Speaking

To aim for Grade A, try to do this in every answer:

  • Speak for 30–60 seconds (not just one sentence).
  • Use two tenses (past + present or future).
  • Use linking words: because, so, but, however, although, first, then, finally…
  • Use stronger vocabulary, not always basic words (for example: exhausted instead of very tired).
  • React to your partner: ask questions, agree, disagree politely.
  • Self-correct if you notice a mistake (“…went… sorry, I mean I’ve been going…”).

How to use this warm-up

  • Work in pairs or small groups where possible.
  • Speak out loud, not just in your head.
  • Focus on communicating clearly, not being perfect.
  • Move through the pages in order, or jump to the part you need most.

When you feel ready, go to the next page and start with Activity 1.

Activity 1 – Turbo Part 1 Interview

Practise Part 1–style interview questions with extended answers. Take turns in pairs.

How to answer for Grade A

Grade A challenge: for each answer, include:

  • One sentence in the past.
  • One sentence in the present or future.
  • Two linking words (for example: because, so, however).
  • One stronger word (for example: incredible, relaxing, stressful, exciting).

Speak for about 30–60 seconds per question.

Question bank

Family & home
  • Tell me about your family. Who are you closest to? Why?
  • Do you get on well with your family? Why or why not?
  • Where do you live? What do you like and dislike about your area?
Daily routine & school
  • What is a typical school day like for you?
  • Which school subject do you find most challenging? Why?
  • Tell me about a teacher who has influenced you.
Free time & hobbies
  • What do you usually do in your free time?
  • Tell me about a hobby you have enjoyed for a long time.
  • Is there a hobby you would like to start in the future? Why?
Technology & social media
  • How do you use technology in your everyday life?
  • What are the advantages and disadvantages of social media for teenagers?
Travel & experiences
  • Tell me about a trip or day out you really enjoyed.
  • Is there a place you would like to visit one day? Why?

Self-check after each answer

After you speak, quickly check:

  • [ ] I spoke for at least 30 seconds.
  • [ ] I used past + present or future.
  • [ ] I used at least two linking words.
  • [ ] I used one stronger word (for example: exhausted, incredible).

Swap roles: one student asks questions, the other answers. Then change.

Activity 2 – High-Score Language Bank

Upgrade your language with better linking words, comparisons, and speculating phrases.

Linking ideas

Adding

also, as well, moreover, besides

Contrasting

but, however, although, on the other hand

Giving reasons and results

because, so, therefore, as a result

Organising your answer

first, then, after that, finally, in conclusion

Useful structures

Comparing
  • I prefer … to … because …
  • … is more interesting than …
  • … is less relaxing than …
Talking about frequency
  • I usually / often / sometimes / hardly ever / rarely / never …
Speculating (guessing)
  • He might be … / She could be …
  • They probably feel …
  • It seems like …

Mini-task (pairs)

  1. Student A gives a simple answer to a question.
  2. Student B helps to upgrade it using:
    • two new linking words,
    • one comparative (for example: more interesting than),
    • one speculation (for example: might / could / probably).

Say the “normal” answer first, then the “upgraded” answer.

Try to use at least one new linking word in your next answer today.

Activity 3 – Part 2 Photo Description

Practise a clear, high-score structure for describing people and situations in a photo.

5-step structure for Part 2

  1. Introduction – “In this picture I can see …”
  2. Place & people – “It looks like they are in … There are some / a group of …”
  3. Actions (present continuous) – “They are sitting / talking / studying / playing …”
  4. Feelings & atmosphere – “They seem / look happy, bored, stressed, excited… The atmosphere is peaceful / noisy …”
  5. Speculation & opinion – “Maybe they are … They might be … In my opinion, it’s a great / stressful situation because …”

Useful vocabulary (B1+)

Feelings

relaxed, nervous, disappointed, confused, enthusiastic, proud, worried

Atmosphere

noisy, peaceful, crowded, busy, comfortable, tense

Activities

revising for an exam, practising a sport, hanging out with friends, doing group work, waiting for a train or bus

No photo? Imagination practice

  1. Student A invents a photo (for example: “a group of students in a library”).
  2. Student A describes it using the 5-step structure.
  3. Student B listens and then says what they imagine:
    • “So in your picture, the students are …”
    • “The atmosphere sounds …”

Grade A extra: include at least 3 present continuous verbs, 2 feelings words, and 1 speculation phrase (might / could / probably).

Speak for about one minute when you describe your imaginary photo.

Activity 4 – Part 3 Discussion & Decision

Practise discussing options with a partner and choosing one together, using natural phrases.

Key phrases for Part 3

Making suggestions

Why don’t we…? · We could… · How about…? · Another idea is to…

Reacting to ideas

That’s a good idea because… · I’m not sure about that because… · I see your point, but… · Maybe, but I think…

Asking your partner

What do you think about…? · Do you agree? · Which option do you prefer?

Reaching a decision

So, shall we choose…? · We both agree that… · Let’s decide on… because…

Practice topic – “English Day” at school

Situation: Your school is planning a special “English Day”. Discuss these ideas and choose the best one:

  • Watching an English film
  • Inviting an English-speaking guest
  • Organising a quiz competition
  • Doing role-plays and drama
  • Having an international food day

Instructions:

  1. Talk about all the options.
  2. Give reasons for each opinion.
  3. Ask your partner’s opinion at least twice.
  4. Agree on one final choice.

Grade A challenge checklist

  • [ ] I asked my partner at least two questions.
  • [ ] I reacted to my partner (I agree / I don’t agree / I see your point…).
  • [ ] I helped to make a clear final decision.
  • [ ] I used at least three different phrases from the box.

Change the topic and repeat (for example: best school trip, best hobby for teenagers, best way to relax).

Activity 5 – Grammar Booster: Mix Your Tenses

High-level B1 answers usually mix tenses. Practise joining past, present, and future.

1. Past + present

Use the past to say when something started, and the present to show it is still true.

  • I started playing tennis when I was ten, and now I play every weekend.
  • Last year I went to Spain and now I want to travel more.

2. Present + future

Use the present for now and the future to show your plans.

  • I study English three times a week because I want to work abroad in the future.
  • I live in a small town, but I am going to move to the city when I go to university.

Task – upgrade simple answers

Take these simple answers and make them longer Grade A answers:

  1. I play football.
  2. I like English.
  3. Last weekend I stayed at home.

For each one, include:

  • past + present or present + future,
  • one linking word (for example: because, so, but),
  • one clear reason.

Say your upgraded answer to your partner and ask: “What do you think?”

Use this technique in your exam: always connect your ideas across different times.

Activity 6 – Fluency & Pronunciation Sprint

Now focus on speaking smoothly and sounding confident.

1. 30-second monologue

Choose one topic:

  • My typical weekend
  • A person I admire
  • My plans for next year
  • The best day I can remember

Speak for 30 seconds without stopping. Concentrate on:

  • speaking smoothly (don’t worry about a few mistakes),
  • using linking words: and, so, because, then, after that, finally.

Then repeat:

  • try to self-correct one mistake you remember,
  • add one new stronger word (for example: amazing, exhausting, relaxing).

2. Stress & intonation – sound more natural

Read these sentences aloud. Make the bold words stronger:

  • I really enjoy playing the guitar.
  • It was quite difficult at first, but now it’s much easier.
  • I’d probably choose the film because it’s more relaxing.

Partner:

  • listen and say if they sound natural and confident,
  • then swap roles.

In the exam, focus on keeping your voice clear and steady rather than fast.

Final Reminder – Walk In Switched On

Before you go into your B1 Speaking exam, quickly review these points in your mind.

For Grade A in B1 Speaking

  • Don’t give short answers – develop your ideas with examples.
  • Use more than one tense and good linking words.
  • Show interest in your partner – listen, react, ask questions.
  • If you make a mistake, don’t panic – correct yourself and continue.
  • Use some stronger vocabulary to sound more precise and natural.

Last encouragement

You have already started the exam in the best way: by warming up. Trust your preparation, listen carefully, and enjoy the chance to talk.
