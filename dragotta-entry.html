Welcome

This test is not an exam. It is a way for your teacher to see how you communicate in English in situations that are familiar to you, and then in similar situations when you are travelling.

How to complete this test

  • Answer in simple, clear English.
  • Do not worry about making mistakes. Focus on sharing your ideas.
  • You can answer by speaking or writing, depending on your teacher’s instructions.
  • Take your time with each page before you move on.

When you are ready, go to the next page to begin the first task.

Everyday life: typical day

First, talk about your daily life during the week.

1. Daily life

Describe a typical day in your life during the week. What do you usually do?

Try to use full sentences. Imagine you are telling a new friend about your routine.

Travel: typical day on holiday

Now think about a similar day when you are travelling.

1. Holiday day

Imagine you are on holiday in another country. Describe a typical day on your trip.

You can talk about where you go, what you see, what you eat, and who you meet.

Everyday life: small problem

Everyone has small problems from time to time. Here you describe one of yours.

2. Everyday problem

Describe a small problem you had recently (for example: with a bill, an appointment, or something you bought).

What happened, and how did you solve it?

Focus on telling the story clearly: what went wrong and what you did.

Travel: problem abroad

Now imagine a similar problem while you are travelling.

2. Problem abroad

Imagine a similar problem happens while you are abroad (for example: at a hotel or with a train ticket).

What would you say to explain the problem and ask for help?

Think about how you would stay calm and make sure the other person understands the situation.

Everyday life: favourite place

Next, talk about a place you enjoy in your own town or city.

3. Favourite place

Talk about a place in your town or city that you really like. Why do you like it?

You can talk about what you do there, how it looks or feels, and why it is special to you.

Travel: place you want to visit

Finally in this section, think about a place you would like to visit in another country.

3. Place to visit

Talk about a place in another country that you would like to visit one day. Why do you want to go there?

You can mention what you know about this place and what you would like to see or do there.

Functional language: in a café

Now you will practise short, useful sentences for common situations.

Situation 1 – In a café

You are in a café (at home or in another country). You want to order a coffee politely.

What would you say?

Think about a polite way to order, as if you are speaking to the person at the counter.

Functional language: in a shop

This situation also works in an airport or station shop.

Situation 2 – In a shop or supermarket

You cannot find an item, but you think the shop sells it.

What would you say to the shop assistant?

Imagine you are standing in the aisle and need help to find the item.

Functional language: when you do not understand

Here you ask someone to repeat or explain something again.

Situation 3 – Not understanding

You did not understand what someone just said (in a conversation, on the phone, or while travelling).

What would you say?

Try to be polite and clear so the other person is happy to repeat or explain.

Functional language: polite request

Now you ask someone to do something for you in a polite way.

Situation 4 – Request

You are in a waiting room and you are cold. The window is open.

What would you say to the other person?

Think of a sentence you would feel comfortable using with a stranger.

Functional language: making a booking

Finally in this section, you make a simple booking.

Situation 5 – Booking

You want to book a time for something (for example: a haircut, a table in a restaurant, a lesson, and so on).

Imagine you are phoning or speaking to someone at the desk.

What would you say to make the booking?

Include what you want to book, for when, and any other important details.

Explaining problems: everyday life

In the last part of the test, you explain two situations. This one is from everyday life.

Task 1 – Online order

You ordered something online and it never arrived.

Write or say three to five sentences explaining:

  • what you ordered
  • what the problem is
  • what you want the company to do (refund, send it again, and so on)

Imagine you are writing an email or speaking on the phone.

Try to be clear and polite so that the company understands the problem and can help you.

Explaining problems: travel

Now imagine a similar kind of problem when you are travelling.

Task 2 – Lost bag at the airport

You are at an airport in another country and your bag is missing.

Write or say three to five sentences explaining:

  • what happened
  • what your bag looks like (colour, size, and so on)
  • what you want the staff to do (check, call you, deliver it, and so on)

Try to use similar language to Task 1, but adapted to this travel situation.
